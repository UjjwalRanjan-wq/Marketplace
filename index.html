🧭 Executive Summary — What the Data Is Telling Us

Across 33,540+ seller feedback signals from ASUS (May 2024–Mar 2026) and Reddit (Jun 2025–Mar 2026), three dominant pain themes account for over 55% of all volume:

  • 🔴 Item Setup & Catalog (8,198 signals, 24%) — Adding items, bulk uploads, publication delays, and catalog sync are the #1 seller pain point by a wide margin.
  • 🔴 Platform UX & Stability (3,064 signals, 9%) — Page crashes, confusing navigation, and error opacity frustrate sellers daily; partially covered by 27020.
  • 🔴 Support Response Quality (2,934 signals, 9%) — Slow/unhelpful support is #3 overall. The 1,639 "slow/unhelpful" subtopic signals represent an acute gap — AOP initiatives 27019 & 27044 address tooling but not response quality SLAs.
  • 🟡 Orders & Fulfillment (2,801 signals, 8%) — Mostly covered by AOP (27002, 27016, 27017, 27027, 27036).
  • 🟡 Emerging: Seller Risk & Suspension (95 Reddit posts, accelerating) — Compliance reviews, delisting, and suspension transparency have no direct AOP coverage and are trending up on Reddit.

🚨 High-Priority Feedback Gaps — No AOP Coverage
GAP #1: Support Response Quality & SLA Transparency
📊 1,639 ASUS signals + 72 Reddit posts · #3 overall issue · Trend: ▲ Rising

Sellers overwhelmingly cite slow, unhelpful support responses as a top pain. AOP initiatives 27019 (Agent Productivity/UCM) and 27044 (Marty AI Self-Service) address the tooling layer but do NOT address response quality SLAs, seller-facing communication, or escalation pathways.

💬 Evidence: "I've been waiting 3 weeks for a case response" · "Support just closes tickets without resolving" · "No way to escalate urgent issues" · "AI bot doesn't understand my problem"
🟣 Recommendation: Define seller support SLA commitments + seller-visible case status tracking as a new AOP item under Track 6
GAP #2: Notification & Messaging UX
📊 333 ASUS signals (Communications track) · No Reddit signal · Trend: Stable

Sellers report missing, confusing, or delayed platform notifications across: order updates, policy changes, account actions, and compliance notices. This is a distinct UX layer separate from support cases — no AOP item in the Top 50 addresses notification architecture or messaging UX.

💬 Evidence: "I don't get notifications when orders are updated" · "Policy change emails are buried" · "No way to know when my item was unpublished" · "Missing alerts on WFS inbound issues"
🟣 Recommendation: Add notification/communications UX to Track 14A (Platform Experiences) scope or as standalone initiative
GAP #3: Seller Suspension Transparency & Risk Communication
📊 95 Reddit posts (Seller Risk topic) · 45 compliance subtopic · Trend: ▲ +40% MoM — ACCELERATING

Reddit data shows rapidly growing seller frustration with compliance reviews, account suspensions, and delisting with zero transparency. Sellers report no clear explanation of what triggered suspension, no appeal pathway, and poor communication. 27035 (Hazmat Compliance) and 27018 (Limit Losses) are adjacent but don't address the seller-facing communication layer.

💬 Reddit evidence: "Account suspended with no reason given" · "Delisted overnight, no email" · "Compliance review has been open 60 days" · "No way to appeal item removal"
🟣 Recommendation: Seller Risk & Compliance Communication should be a dedicated AOP item under Track 6 (Seller Performance Monitoring — 27037 extension)
GAP #4: Buyer Fraud / Abuse Protection from Seller Perspective
📊 264 ASUS signals + 28 Reddit (Return Related Support) · Trend: Stable

Sellers report systematic buyer abuse patterns: fraudulent returns, false "not received" claims, and chargeback abuse that erodes seller trust. 27018 (Limit Financial Losses) covers Walmart's financial exposure but does NOT address seller-side abuse protections, dispute pathways, or seller communication around suspected fraud.

💬 Evidence: "Buyers claiming packages not received to get free items" · "False return reasons accepted automatically" · "No seller protection against repeated fraudulent buyers"
🟣 Recommendation: Add Seller Protection Program to Track 5B (CBT/Seller Fulfilled) scope — anti-abuse detection + seller dispute pathway

🩹 What Are Paper Cuts?

Paper Cuts are nagging, recurring, high-frequency friction points reported repeatedly by sellers — typically low-to-medium severity individually but with high cumulative annoyance and operational impact. They are the "death by a thousand cuts" issues that don't make it to critical escalations but erode seller trust and platform NPS over time.

Each Paper Cut is scored on 6 weighted dimensions: Recurrence · Breadth · Persistence · Friction Signal · Support Burden · Source Diversity

📐 Paper Cut Scoring Model — Composite Score (0–100)

📊 Recurrence (25%)
Count of mentions across time periods. Normalized against dataset max. Issues with 500+ mentions score 80+.
🌐 Breadth (20%)
Unique sellers/PIDs/geos affected. High breadth (200+ unique sellers) = breadth score 85+. Indicates systemic vs. isolated issue.
📅 Persistence (20%)
Weeks present continuously across data. 52+ weeks = max score. Issues that keep reappearing despite potential fixes.
😤 Friction Signal (20%)
NLP-detected language: "every time," "always fails," "impossible," "annoying," repeated follow-up patterns. High friction = score 80+.
📞 Support Burden (10%)
Case reopen rate, escalation frequency, multi-contact issues. Proxy: Support Hub mention + cross-source confirmation.
🔀 Source Diversity (5%)
Issue confirmed in 2+ source channels (ASUS + Reddit, Doorbell + Support, etc.). Multi-source = highest confidence tier.
